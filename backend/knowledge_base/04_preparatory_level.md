# Preparatory Level

## Suitable Age Group

The Preparatory Level is generally suitable for children aged 8 years onwards.

## Structure and Duration

The Preparatory Level includes 4 levels, with each level lasting approximately 4 months.

## Purpose

This stage is designed to strengthen the child’s operational fluency and connect abacus learning with school mathematics.

At this stage, children move from basic number handling to stronger calculation ability, visual strategies, and school-aligned problem solving.

## What Children Learn

The Preparatory Level may include:

- Multiplication
- Division
- Decimal operations
- Word problems
- BODMAS
- Mental maths techniques
- Abacus-based calculation fluency
- Visual picture-memory development
- Faster and more accurate operations
- Application of mathematical concepts in school-style problems

## How It Helps Children

This level helps children:

- Strengthen arithmetic confidence
- Calculate faster and more accurately
- Improve school mathematics performance
- Build mental picture memory
- Understand multiplication and division better
- Develop discipline through regular practice
- Handle word problems with more confidence

## Parent-Friendly Explanation

The Preparatory Level is useful for children who already know basic numbers and are ready to build calculation power. The program helps them understand arithmetic operations visually, practice regularly, and improve school performance.

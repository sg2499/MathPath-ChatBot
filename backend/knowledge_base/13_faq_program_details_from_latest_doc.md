# MathPath FAQ Details

## What is Math Path Abacus?

MathPath Abacus helps children fall in love with maths by making it simple, visual, and fun. The program is designed from UKG to Class 8 and combines abacus learning, cognitive training, visualisation techniques, and school syllabus integration.

## Entry Programs for Different Age Groups

MathPath has three entry programs based on the child’s class group:

### Young Learner

For UKG, Class 1, and Class 2.

Duration: 10 months.

Learning focus: foundational operations in abacus.

After completion, the child is promoted to Preparatory Level 2.

### Preparatory Level 1

For Class 3 and Class 4.

Duration: 4 months.

Learning focus: visual and operational fluency.

After completion, the child is promoted to Preparatory Level 2.

### Bridge Course

For Class 5 to Class 8 late joiners.

Duration: 10–12 months.

Four levels are clubbed into one compact level.

After completion, the child is promoted to Intermediate Level 1.

## Total Program Duration

The full journey is approximately 4 years, depending on the child’s entry level and pace of level completion.

## How MathPath Helps Children Improve in Maths

MathPath works on concentration, whole-brain development, visual memory, picture memory, number sense, and calculation confidence. Numbers are used as a medium of training and communication, and every class includes a brain-boosting happy session to make maths engaging.

## Class Frequency and Duration

MathPath provides one class per week for 2 hours. Monthly, 4 classes are provided. Students also submit daily practice sheets through the automated student portal.

## Batch Size

Each batch allows a maximum of 10–12 students for proper attention and balanced time distribution.

## Academic Session and Admission

MathPath does not follow a fixed academic session for new admission. Admissions happen round the year. A child’s learning starts from the day of joining, and assessments happen based on individual level completion, not group completion.

## Competitions

MathPath conducts annual competitions. Students from Level 2 onwards are eligible. The event is conducted as a full-day event, with result declaration and prize distribution on the same day.

## Class Day Options

MathPath classes are mainly after-school activities. Weekday classes are offered from 5 PM onwards. Weekend classes are offered on Saturday and Sunday in morning, afternoon, and evening batches. Exact availability should be confirmed with the MathPath team.

## Assessment and Certification

Every level has a level-end assessment. A minimum score of 75% is required for promotion to the next level. After successful assessment, a certificate is issued for every level.

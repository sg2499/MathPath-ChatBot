# Latest MathPath FAQ Knowledge Base

## What is MathPath Abacus?

MathPath Abacus helps children fall in love with maths by making it simple, visual, and fun. The program is designed from UKG to Class 8 and combines abacus mastery, visualisation techniques, cognitive training, and school-syllabus integration.

## Entry Programs by Age / Class Group

MathPath has three entry programs:

- Young Learner – For UKG, Class 1, and Class 2. Duration: 10 months. Learning focus: foundational operations in abacus. After completion, the child is promoted to Preparatory Level 2.
- Preparatory Level 1 – For Class 3 and Class 4. Duration: 4 months. Learning focus: visual and operational fluency. After completion, the child is promoted to Preparatory Level 2.
- Bridge Course – For Class 5 to Class 8 late joiners. Duration: 10–12 months. Four levels are clubbed into one compact level. After completion, the child is promoted to Intermediate Level 1.

## Total Program Duration

The total MathPath journey is approximately 4 years, depending on the child’s entry level and pace of level completion.

## How MathPath Helps Children Improve in Maths

MathPath works on concentration, whole-brain development, visual memory, picture memory, number sense, calculation confidence, speed, accuracy, and regular practice habits. Numerical learning is used as a medium of training and communication.

## Brain Spark / Brain Boost Happy Session

Each class includes a 30-minute Brain Spark / Brain Boost happy session so children can learn maths in a fun way.

## Class Frequency and Duration

MathPath provides one class per week. Each class is 2 hours long. Monthly 4 classes are provided. This is followed by daily practice sheet submission in the automated student portal.

## Batch Size

Maximum 10–12 students are allowed in each batch for proper attention and balanced time distribution.

## Admission Session

There is no fixed academic session for admission. Admissions happen round the year. Children start learning from the day they join, and assessments happen based on individual level completion, not as a fixed group.

## Competitions

MathPath conducts annual competitions. Students from Level 2 onwards are eligible to participate. It is a full-day event, and result declaration and prize distribution happen on the same day.

## Class Day Options

Abacus classes are mainly after-school activities. Weekday classes are available from 5 PM onward. Weekend classes are available on Saturday and Sunday in morning, afternoon, and evening batches.

## Assessment and Certification

Every level has a level-end assessment. A minimum score of 75% is required for promotion to the next level. A certificate is issued after successful assessment for every level.

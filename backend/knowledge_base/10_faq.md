# MathPath FAQ

## What is MathPath?

MathPath is a hybrid abacus and visualisation-based mathematics learning program for children. It helps children improve mathematical foundations, calculation speed, accuracy, confidence, and school maths application.

## What is the full name or tagline?

MathPath is positioned as **MathPath — Ace with Abacus**.

## What age group is MathPath for?

MathPath supports children from approximately LKG/UKG to Grade 7. Specific placement depends on the child’s age, class, and existing mathematical foundation.

## Is MathPath only for abacus?

No. MathPath includes abacus, visualisation, mental maths, school syllabus integration, Daily Practice Sheets, app-based practice, assessments, report cards, and practical maths topics depending on the level.

## Which boards does MathPath support?

MathPath is designed to support school mathematics needs across CBSE, ICSE, WBSEB, and State Board patterns.

## Does MathPath offer online classes?

MathPath follows a hybrid learning model. Parents should contact the MathPath team for current online/offline batch options and timings.

## What is the Young Learner Program?

The Young Learner Program is for children around 5–7 years. It focuses on number sense, place value, abacus basics, simple operations, Golden Step Formula, complements, and visualisation.

## What is the Preparatory Level?

The Preparatory Level is generally for children aged 8 years onwards. It includes 4 levels of around 4 months each and covers multiplication, division, decimals, BODMAS, word problems, and mental picture memory.

## What is the Intermediate Level?

The Intermediate Level is for children ready for advanced application. It includes complex multiplication and division, decimals, integers, squares, BODMAS, strategic thinking, and middle-school readiness.

## What is the Master Module?

The Master Module is an advanced module covering percentages, square roots, cubes and cube roots, profit and loss, decimal operations, and simple interest.

## What is the Bridge Course?

The Bridge Course is MathPath’s special support for late joiners, especially older children who need to build foundations quickly and enter the right learning pathway without unnecessary repetition.

## Does MathPath help children weak in maths?

Yes. MathPath is designed to strengthen fundamentals, number sense, calculation confidence, visualisation, and practice habits. It can help children who are weak in basics become more confident over time.

## Does MathPath help with Olympiad readiness?

MathPath strengthens calculation speed, accuracy, logic, and confidence, which can support Olympiad readiness. Exact preparation details should be confirmed with the MathPath team.

## Are there assessments?

Yes. MathPath includes mock tests, level assessments, report cards, and progress tracking.

## Are parents updated?

Yes. The system supports parent feedback, progress tracking, report cards, and missed-practice alerts.

# Young Learner Program

## Suitable Age Group

The Young Learner Program is suitable for children aged approximately 5–7 years.

## Duration

The program duration is approximately 10 months.

## Purpose

This program is designed for early learners who are beginning their mathematics journey. At this stage, children need joyful, concrete, and visual experiences to understand numbers.

The goal is to build a strong foundation before the child moves into more advanced abacus and school maths concepts.

## What Children Learn

The Young Learner Program may include:

- Number sense
- Number recognition
- Place value
- Comparing numbers
- Simple addition
- Simple subtraction
- Abacus basics
- Use of beads and fingers
- Golden Step Formula
- Complements of 5
- Complements of 10
- Visualisation skills
- Introduction to multiplication readiness
- Early mental picture memory

## How It Helps Children

This program helps young children:

- Stop fearing numbers
- Understand numbers visually
- Build confidence in basic calculations
- Develop finger and bead coordination
- Improve concentration
- Develop memory and observation
- Prepare for school maths more confidently

## Parent-Friendly Explanation

For young children, MathPath does not rush into difficult calculations. It first builds number comfort, place value understanding, and visual number sense. The abacus acts as a concrete tool, and slowly the child begins to imagine numbers mentally.
